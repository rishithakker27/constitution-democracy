## 🔑 Key Results

### 1. Machine Learning Can Predict Democracy from Constitutional Text

The headline finding is that **constitutional text alone can predict how democratic a country is** — with surprising accuracy:

| Model | Variables Used | R² |
|-------|---------------|-----|
| OLS Regression | 10 provisions | 0.17 |
| Gradient Boosting | 10 provisions | 0.40 |
| CatBoost | 51 provisions | **0.79** |
| Random Forest | 51 provisions | **0.79** |

This means ~79% of the variation in real-world democracy scores can be explained just by reading a country's constitution. The relationship is highly **non-linear** — simple regression misses most of it.

---

### 2. The Most Important Constitutional Provisions

These features consistently topped the charts across **all 5 democracy indices**:

| Rank | Provision | What It Means |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| 🥇 1 | **`partprf`** — Party/candidate restrictions | Can people freely form parties & compete? (~10% importance) |
| 🥈 2 | **`lhterm`** — Lower house term length | Shorter terms = more accountable legislature |
| 🥉 3 | **`censor`** — Censorship prohibition | Anti-censorship clauses strongly predict democracy |
| 4 | **`offrel`** — Official state religion | Secular states score consistently higher |
| 5 | **`votemin`** — Voting age minimum | Lower voting age → more democratic |
| 6 | **`speedtri`** — Right to speedy trial | Due process protections matter |
| 7 | **`amndapct`** — Amendment threshold | How hard it is to change the constitution |
| 8 | **`infoacc`** — Freedom of information | Transparency provisions matter |

> **Bottom line:** Freedom of political competition, free press, and secular governance are the constitutional provisions most predictive of real democracy.

---

### 3. Which Type of Democracy is Most "Constitutional"?

The models performed best predicting **egalitarian democracy** and worst on **participatory democracy**:

| V-Dem Index | R² |
|-------------|-----|
| Egalitarian Democracy | **0.827** ← best predicted |
| Deliberative Democracy | 0.799 |
| Electoral Democracy | 0.789 |
| Liberal Democracy | 0.758 |
| Participatory Democracy | **0.720** ← hardest to predict |

Constitutional text is especially good at predicting whether political power is **equally distributed** — but participatory democracy (civil society, grassroots organizing) depends more on factors outside the constitution.

---

### 4. Four Types of Constitutions (LLM Clustering)

GPT scored 192 constitutions across 10 dimensions, and K-Means found **4 natural clusters**:

| Cluster | Size | Profile | Example Countries |
|---------|------|---------|------------------|
| 🟢 **"Full Democracy"** | 65 | Near-perfect scores across all 10 dimensions | South Africa, Spain, Poland, Brazil, Philippines |
| 🔵 **"Moderate Liberal"** | 56 | Strong rule of law, weak socioeconomic/military control | USA, Canada, Germany, Norway, Japan, India |
| 🟡 **"Mixed/Procedural"** | 70 | Moderate across the board — many hybrid regimes | France, Sweden, Denmark, China, Turkey, Iran |
| 🔴 **"Minimal"** | 5 | Very low on nearly everything | Saudi Arabia, Brunei + historical states |

> ⚠️ **Interesting anomaly:** Most of Western Europe (France, Sweden, Denmark) falls in the "Mixed" cluster — likely because their constitutions are **older and shorter**, scoring lower on text breadth even though those countries are highly democratic in practice. This highlights the gap between constitutional *text* and democratic *reality*.

---

### 5. The Big Takeaway

> **Constitutional design matters — but it's complicated.**
> - The provisions that matter most are **political competition, free press, and secular governance** — not abstract rights declarations
> - Countries with **newer, more comprehensive constitutions** (many post-1990 Global South democracies) score high on paper but vary in practice
> - Old, terse constitutions (USA, Western Europe) score lower on text breadth despite being highly democratic — showing the **limits of text-based analysis**
> - Simple linear regression massively **underestimates** how much constitutions matter — you need ML to capture the full relationship
